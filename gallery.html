Plaster of paris

 | plaster, steel, video camera | 

2000

... in which my figure has an inwardly immersed and dissolved corporeality, the idea arises of something invisible which belongs to the past, and compared to which the visible figure is something left over. The animal motives are important to me because they are eternally inexhaustible and infinite, they belong to the first motives of mankind, and they give a connection to our unconscious, to what can be felt but not grasped.
